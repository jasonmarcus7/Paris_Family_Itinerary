Day by Day

Arrival & Le Marais

Arrival · Coffee · Discovery
Arrival: Paris · 10h45  ·  Transfer to hotel — luggage storage available if room not yet ready
Gluten Free · Dairy Free · Every Day All restaurant and café recommendations throughout this itinerary are either 100% dedicated gluten-free and dairy-free establishments, or venues widely verified as safe. GF · DF tags appear on each entry. Always confirm your requirements when booking.
  • 10h45
    Arrival in Paris Transfer to hotel in the Marais or 11th arrondissement — the ideal base for all four days. Drop bags and take stock of the neighbourhood before heading out.
  • 12h00
    Lunch — Noglu, Passage des Panoramas (2nd) GF · DF One of Paris's most celebrated 100% gluten-free café-bakeries, Noglu has been a veteran of the Paris GF scene for over ten years. Their GF ham and cheese baguette is exceptional, the pistachio eclair extraordinary. Pain au chocolat, croissants, avocado toast, and hot sandwiches round out a brilliant lunch. Dairy-free options clearly marked. Arrive early or book ahead — the reputation is well earned.
  • 14h00
    Place des Vosges & Marais Wander Paris's oldest and most beautiful square — arcaded Renaissance walkways, sandstone façades, and a garden where the children can run. From here, wander the medieval lanes of the Marais at a Sunday pace — past galleries, boutiques, and hidden courtyards leading toward the 11th.
  • 15h00
    Maison Plume — Afternoon Pastry GF · DF A stunning patisserie in Le Marais where every pastry is gluten-free, dairy-free, and refined sugar-free. The signature feather-shaped plume pastries are filled with luscious cream and are among the most beautiful GF patisserie in Paris. A worthy and rather delicious afternoon stop.
  • 16h30
    Atelier des Lumières — Immersive Art A former iron foundry transformed into Europe's largest digital art centre — floor-to-ceiling projections of Monet, Klimt, or Van Gogh filling every surface including the floor. One of the most genuinely awe-inspiring experiences in Paris for all ages. The children will be completely transfixed. Book tickets in advance online.
  • 19h30
    Dinner — Aujourd'hui Demain, 11th Arrondissement GF · DF A stylish concept café-restaurant in the 11th where everything is 100% plant-based and entirely gluten-free — all made from scratch including plant milks and GF breads. A bright, minimalist interior with reclaimed wood and abundant greenery, welcoming and relaxed for families. One of the most acclaimed fully GF/DF dining experiences in Paris.
Recommended Stay — Marais or 11th arrondissement boutique hotel
Central location · Walking distance to all Sunday and Monday activities · Easy RER C access for Versailles on Tuesday
1 Sun Arrival

Louvre & Tour Eiffel

Art · Icons · River
  • 09h00
    Breakfast — Noglu, Rue Basfroi (11th) or Rue de Grenelle (7th) GF · DF Noglu's 11th arrondissement location on Rue Basfroi opens from 08h30 and is ideal if staying in the Marais. Avocado toast, the famous egg sandwich, croissants, and Buddha bowls for breakfast. The 7th arrondissement location on Rue de Grenelle is equally cosy and convenient for the Eiffel Tower end of the day.
  • 10h00
    Jardin des Tuileries — Morning Stroll A gentle walk through the formal gardens linking the Louvre to Place de la Concorde. The central round ponds have toy boat rentals from mid-morning — a quintessential Parisian pleasure for young children and a calm start to the day before the museum.
  • 11h30
    Coffee — Télescope, Rue Villedo (1st) GF · DF Five minutes from the Louvre, one of Paris's benchmark specialty coffee shops — minimalist, serious, beloved by the city's coffee cognoscenti. Black coffees and dairy-free milks available. A perfect pre-Louvre pause.
  • 12h30
    The Louvre — Timed Entry ✦ Confirmed With curious children aged 8 and 10, focus rather than exhaust: the Egyptian Antiquities rooms are extraordinary — mummies, sarcophagi, hieroglyphs. Then the Winged Victory of Samothrace on the grand staircase. Collect a free family trail booklet at the information desk. Allow 2 to 2.5 hours.
  • 15h00
    Late Lunch — CoCoCo, near Palais Royal (1st) GF · DF An exceptional find near the Palais Royal — a Japanese-style bento box lunch restaurant that is 100% gluten-free, organic, and lactose-free, all homemade. The bento format is great fun for children — a generous box with rice, pickled vegetables, a main, and various accompaniments. The Japanese beef curry and the karaagé fried chicken are standout mains. Lunch only; book ahead.
  • 16h30
    Tour Eiffel — Timed Climb ✦ Must See Book timed entry tickets in advance — the queues for walk-ins are substantial. The second floor at 115m gives extraordinary panoramic views and is accessible by lift; the summit at 276m is exhilarating for older children. Allow 1.5 hours. The experience of Paris laid out below, in every direction, is one the children will carry for years.
  • 18h30
    Champ de Mars — Evening & Light Show Settle on the Champ de Mars lawn as Paris settles into evening. The tower's famous five-minute sparkling light show begins on the hour after dark — the children will insist on watching it more than once. A naturally gluten-free and dairy-free moment: a Berthillon sorbet or street crêpe (check ingredients) in hand.
  • 20h00
    Dinner — Wild & the Moon, 1st or 8th Arrondissement GF · DF A celebrated Parisian chain of modern plant-based eateries — all food 100% gluten-free, dairy-free, organic, and vegan. Superbowls, salads, soups, bahn mi, and baked goods with genuine flavour. Several central locations; the 1st or 8th work perfectly for a post-Eiffel dinner. Casual, fast, and genuinely very good.
Highlight Experience
The Tour Eiffel at Dusk

Book the late afternoon slot deliberately. Ascending as the sun sets over Paris — the Seine catching the last light, the Sacré-Cœur glowing on the hill to the north, the city extending to every horizon — is one of the great travel experiences. Descending to watch the sparkling light show from the Champ de Mars grass, the tower illuminated above, is the kind of memory that lasts a lifetime.

2 Mon Louvre

Versailles Trianon · Notre-Dame

Palace · Hamlet · Cathedral
  • 08h30
    Breakfast — Judy Café GF · DF Judy is a fully gluten-free café owned by an Australian naturopath, with three central Paris locations. All-day brunch menu: GF/DF pancakes, avocado toast, eggs, banana bread, and excellent coffee with dairy-free milk options. A grounding and nourishing start before the longest day of the trip. Take a GF pastry to go for the train if time is short.
  • 09h15
    RER C from Rive Gauche — to Versailles Château Board the RER C from any Left Bank station — Saint-Michel Notre-Dame, Musée d'Orsay, or Invalides — direct to Versailles Château Rive Gauche. Approximately 35 to 40 minutes. Note this same line and station for the return journey, which passes directly past the Île de la Cité and Notre-Dame.
  • 11h00
    Arrive Versailles — Walk Through the Gardens to Trianon Enter through the main Palace gates and walk directly into the grand formal gardens. Head westward through the gardens toward the Trianon domain — the Grand Canal stretches to the left, the formal parterres extend on all sides, the scale is extraordinary. Allow 30 minutes at a leisurely pace — the walk itself is one of the finest in France.
  • 12h00
    Trianon Estate Opens — Grand Trianon & Petit Trianon The entire Trianon domain opens at 12h00. Enter directly from the gardens path. Explore the Grand Trianon — Louis XIV's intimate pink marble retreat, filled with light and remarkable furniture — then the Petit Trianon, the neoclassical gem built for Louis XV and most famously associated with Marie-Antoinette. Access to the Hamlet is via the Petit Trianon gate.
  • 13h00
    The Queen's Hamlet — Marie-Antoinette's Fairytale Village A hamlet of thatched Norman cottages around a still lake — with a working farm and animals, a dovecote, a mill, and winding paths through the most enchanting corner of the entire Versailles estate. For children aged 8 and 10 this is the undisputed highlight of the day. Allow a full, unhurried hour. The afternoon quiet here is extraordinary.
  • 13h45
    Petit Train — Trianon back to the Palace Board the petit train from the Grand Trianon stop for the return to the Palace — approximately 15 to 20 minutes. This gives a comfortable buffer before the 14h00 Palace entry. The children will love the train as much as anything else in Versailles.
  • 14h00
    Palace of Versailles — Timed Entry ✦ Confirmed Enter through the main Cour d'Honneur and proceed through the King's State Apartments to the Hall of Mirrors — 73 metres of gilded arches, painted ceilings, and 357 mirrors overlooking the gardens. One of the great rooms of Europe. The children's audio guide is excellent and available at the information desk. Allow 2 hours. Note: no food service inside the Palace — ensure everyone is well fed beforehand.
  • 16h15
    RER C Return — Alight at Notre-Dame Board the RER C from Versailles Château Rive Gauche toward Paris and alight at Saint-Michel Notre-Dame. The journey is approximately 40 minutes. Notre-Dame de Paris reopened in December 2024 after five years of restoration following the 2019 fire — the restored interior is by all accounts extraordinary and deeply moving. Allow 45 minutes to walk around the exterior and visit the nave.
  • 17h30
    Île Saint-Louis — Evening Stroll & Berthillon Cross from Notre-Dame onto the Île Saint-Louis — one of the most unchanged streets in Paris. Berthillon, the legendary glacier, is here. Their sorbets are fruit-based and naturally dairy-free — flavours change seasonally. A perfect way to close a very full and wonderful day before the short journey back to the hotel.
  • 19h30
    Dinner — Kapunka Thai, multiple locations GF · DF A fully gluten-free Thai restaurant group with three Paris locations — widely praised as one of the finest GF/DF dining options in the city. Pad thai, curries, chicken satay, all cooked in a dedicated gluten-free kitchen. The Khao-Soy red curry and the pad thai are particular highlights. Generous portions, outstanding flavour, and great fun for children. Book ahead — popular and intimate in size.
Highlight Experience
The Queen's Hamlet & Notre-Dame

Two of the most quietly profound places in France, in a single day. The Hamlet at Versailles — a village of thatched cottages and a working farm built by a queen who wanted to feel human again — followed by Notre-Dame, newly risen and more luminous than before the fire. For two curious children, this day will generate more questions than any other. Answer them slowly, and carry the conversation into dinner.

3 Tue Versailles

Montmartre & Departure

Morning · Departure 12h30
  • 08h00
    Breakfast — Café Mareva, Montmartre (18th) GF · DF A completely gluten-free café a few blocks from Sacré-Cœur, serving sweet and savoury waffles made from scratch, pancakes, poke bowls, and loaded toasts on homemade GF bread. Their sweet potato waffles — topped with guacamole, eggs, bacon, or maple syrup — are famous in the Paris GF community. Dairy-free milk available. Open from 09h00; arrive early for the best selection.
  • 09h15
    Sacré-Cœur & the Village of Montmartre Climb the steps to Sacré-Cœur early, before the crowds gather — the view over Paris from the forecourt is one of the finest in the city, the white dome brilliant in the morning light. Then descend through the village lanes: the old vineyard, the traditional windmills, the Place du Tertre where artists have painted since the days of Picasso, Utrillo, and Modigliani.
  • 10h30
    Musée de Montmartre — Renoir's Studio A small, beautiful museum in the oldest house on the Butte — Renoir's former studio, with terraced gardens overlooking the vineyard. The children can stand in the room where some of the most recognised paintings in the world were made. Creative, intimate, and perfectly suited to curious 8 and 10 year olds. Allow 45 minutes.
  • 11h30
    Hotel Checkout & Transfer Collect bags from the hotel — most will hold them from the morning — and transfer to the departure point with time to spare for 12h30. A final sorbet or pastry from Copains Bakery (Marais branch, fully GF/DF) en route if time and appetite allow.
  • 12h30
    Departure Three nights, four unforgettable neighbourhoods, two palaces, one cathedral, one iron tower, and a great many exceptional coffees. À bientôt, Paris.

Paris rewards the curious — and two children aged 8 and 10 are among the finest travelling companions this city could hope for. Take it at their pace, follow their questions, and let the city do the rest.
